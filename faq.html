Frequently Asked Questions

Comprehensive questions and answers about Coast FIRE
What is Coast FIRE?

Coast FIRE (Financial Independence Retire Early) means saving aggressively in your early career until you have enough invested that compound interest alone can grow it to your full FIRE number by traditional retirement age. Once you "coast"—that is, stop or slow your savings—you don't need to add another dollar because market growth will keep you on track.

How much do I need to coast?

You need enough invested assets such that, at retirement age (say 65), they will equal your "FIRE number" without any more contributions. That number depends on:

  1. Your annual spending
  2. Your expected investment return (typically 7-8%)
  3. Your withdrawal rate assumption (commonly 4%)

Formula: FIRE Number = Annual Spending ÷ Withdrawal Rate × (1 + Return)^Years Remaining
Use the calculator on this site to find your exact number.

What's the "safe withdrawal rate"?

The 4% rule (or Trinity Study) means withdrawing 4% of your portfolio annually, adjusted for inflation. This has historically worked in many market scenarios. For Coast FIRE, we typically assume:

  • Conservative: 3-3.5%
  • Moderate: 4%
  • Aggressive: 5%
Lower withdrawal rates mean you need a larger portfolio, but also mean more safety margin.

How does Coast FIRE differ from traditional FIRE?

Traditional FIRE:

  • You save aggressively all the way to retirement
  • Your portfolio grows solely through your contributions + returns
  • You stop contributing entirely at retirement

Coast FIRE:

  • You save aggressively in early career
  • You "coast"—stop or reduce savings—by mid-career (age 45-60)
  • Your accumulated portfolio compounds without contributions to reach your target
The key insight: you get financial independence sooner, then ease up on saving.

When can I coast?

Coast eligibility:

  • Portfolio / FIRE Number ≥ 65% and ≤ 100%
  • Your portfolio is on track to reach full FIRE number by age 67 (or your target retirement age)
  • You can sustain withdrawals while coasting

How does the calculator work?

Our calculator asks for:

  • Current assets and liabilities
  • Annual salary and expenses (or savings rate)
  • Expected investment return (7-8%)
  • Withdrawal rate assumption (4% typical)
It then projects:
  1. Your FIRE number based on your spending
  2. Portfolio growth over time with contributions
  3. The age when you reach "coast" eligibility
  4. Total savings needed during coast phase
If you already meet the 65-100% threshold, it shows how much more to save before reaching full retirement.

What if I'm over 60?

If you're over 60, traditional FIRE is often better because:

  • You have fewer years for compounding
  • RMDs (Required Minimum Distribution) start at age 73
  • Social Security claims and withdrawal planning become more critical
However, Coast FIRE can still work if you've already accumulated a large enough portfolio. Check the calculator—you might be coast-ready or almost there.

How do I account for inflation?

All numbers in our calculations are in today's dollars. The calculator assumes:

  • Inflation of ~2% per year
  • Your FIRE number adjusts upward with inflation
  • Your investments outpace inflation over the long term (7-8% nominal returns)
In practice, you'll see your portfolio value and expenses grow in real terms.

Can I use a higher withdrawal rate?

Yes! Higher withdrawal rates reduce your FIRE number:

  • 3% withdrawal: More conservative, longer security
  • 4% withdrawal: Standard (Trinity Study)
  • 5% withdrawal: Aggressive, assumes strong markets
The calculator lets you pick your own rate. Just be realistic—very high withdrawal rates increase sequence-of-returns risk.

What happens during a recession?

During coasting:

  • Your portfolio compounds without contributions
  • Market dips matter less over decades of time horizon
  • You may choose to reduce spending during downturns to preserve capital

Once fully retired:

  • Diversified portfolios (60/40 or better) weather volatility well
  • Social Security and pension income provide stability
  • Consider shifting asset allocation as you approach retirement
Sequence-of-returns risk is real but manageable with proper planning.

What about Social Security?

Timing:

  • You can claim at age 62 (reduced)
  • Full retirement age is typically 66-67
  • Delayed credits add 8% per year until age 70

Coast FIRE strategy:

In Coast FIRE, you can coast on your portfolio for years before claiming Social Security. This maximizes the benefit while delaying taxation.

How do taxes affect my FIRE number?

Taxes are baked into our calculation by default:

  • We assume a portion of your portfolio is in tax-advantaged accounts (IRA/401k)
  • We estimate RMDs start at age 73
  • Social Security benefits are only partially taxable if income exceeds ~$44,000 for couples
For exact planning, consult a tax professional. Our tool gives reasonable estimates.

What investment allocation should I use?

Aggressive (ages 20-40):

  • 90-100% equities
  • Time in market is your friend over decades

Moderate (ages 40-55):

  • 70-80% equities, 20-30% bonds
  • Better risk management as retirement approaches

Conservative (ages 55+):

  • 60-70% equities, 30-40% bonds
  • Preservation becomes more important than growth
The "glide path" concept is key: start aggressive, gradually shift to preservation.

Should I use a robo-advisor or manual investing?

Robo-advisors:

  • Easier (automated rebalancing, low fees)
  • Better for beginners
  • Examples: Betterment, Wealthfront

Manual investing:

  • Cheap (low expense ratios)
  • Makes you aware of your holdings
  • Requires discipline to rebalance
Both can work. For Coast FIRE, most people succeed with either approach.

Is a Roth IRA worth it?

Yes! Tax-free growth is powerful. By maxing out the annual contribution limit ($7,000 in 2026), you create tax-free growth that can last decades. Consider:

  • Roth IRA for retirement savings
  • Backdoor Roth if income exceeds limits
  • Spare change apps (Acorns, Digit) to round up purchases and invest the change
Tax efficiency is a key advantage of Coast FIRE strategy.
