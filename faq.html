Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to all your Coast FIRE questions
What is Coast FIRE?

Coast FIRE means saving aggressively until compound interest alone can grow your portfolio to your full FIRE number by traditional retirement age.

How much do I need to coast?

You need enough invested assets such that, at retirement age (say 65), they will equal your "FIRE number" without any more contributions. That depends on your spending, expected returns (typically 7-8%), and withdrawal rate assumption (commonly 4%).

What's the "safe withdrawal rate"?

The 4% rule means withdrawing 4% of your portfolio annually, adjusted for inflation. For Coast FIRE, we assume:

  • Conservative: 3-3.5%
  • Moderate: 4%
  • Aggressive: 5%
How does Coast FIRE differ from traditional FIRE?

Traditional FIRE: Save aggressively all the way to retirement.

Coast FIRE: Save aggressively in early career, then stop or reduce savings by mid-career while compound growth carries you to your target.

When can I coast?

Coast eligibility:

  • Portfolio / FIRE Number ≥ 65% and ≤ 100%
  • Your portfolio is on track to reach full FIRE number by retirement age
How does the calculator work?

The calculator asks for current assets, annual expenses, expected return (7%), and withdrawal rate (4%). It projects your FIRE target and when you can stop saving.

What if I'm over 60?

If you're over 60, traditional FIRE is often better because you have fewer years for compounding. However, Coast FIRE can still work if you've already accumulated a large enough portfolio.

How do I account for inflation?

All numbers in our calculations are in nominal (today's) dollars. We use pre-inflation figures:

  • Your Annual Expenses input is your current spending power
  • 7-8% returns are historical nominal averages (which already include inflation)
  • 4% withdrawal rate is a traditional rule of thumb for portfolio sustainability

Over a 30-35 year horizon, a 7% nominal return portfolio typically outpaces average inflation, allowing your purchasing power to grow even if you don't save another dollar. That's the Coast FIRE advantage.

Can I use a higher withdrawal rate?

Yes! Higher withdrawal rates reduce your FIRE number, but increase sequence-of-returns risk. The calculator lets you pick your own rate.

What about Social Security?

You can claim at age 62 (reduced), full retirement age (typically 66-67), or delay to 70 for maximum benefit. In Coast FIRE, you may coast on your portfolio for years before claiming Social Security.

What investment allocation should I use?

Ages 20-40: 90-100% equities (time in market is your friend)

Ages 40-55: 70-80% equities, 20-30% bonds

Ages 55+: 60-70% equities, 30-40% bonds (preservation becomes more important)
